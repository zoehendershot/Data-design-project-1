# Which Weather Conditions Lead to the Most Destructive Wildfires

## Why This Matters

Wildfires are becoming more frequent and destructive, putting communities, ecosystems, and air quality at risk. Understanding the conditions that lead to the most severe fires can help improve prevention efforts and better prepare for high-risk situations.

## The Problem

Wildfires are influenced by a combination of environmental and weather-related factors, including temperature, humidity, wind speed, and drought conditions. However, it is not always clear which of these factors contribute most to large, destructive fires. Without this understanding, it is difficult to anticipate when fires are likely to become severe and require greater resources to manage.

## What This Project Does

This project analyzes historical wildfire data to identify how weather conditions and fire risk indicators relate to the size of fires. By examining patterns in temperature, humidity, wind, and drought levels, the analysis highlights which conditions are most strongly associated with larger burned areas.

The goal is to better understand when fires are more likely to become severe, helping inform risk awareness and support more effective decision-making in wildfire management.
